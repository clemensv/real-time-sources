## What It Provides

The GCCIA operates the electricity interconnection grid linking all six GCC states. The interconnection allows power transfer between countries — critical for managing the extreme summer demand peaks when air conditioning drives electricity consumption to extraordinary levels.

Key aspects:
- **Cross-border power flows**: Bilateral and multilateral energy exchange between GCC states
- **System frequency**: Gulf grid operates at 50 Hz (with some 60 Hz sections in Saudi Arabia)
- **Peak demand management**: Summer peaks can exceed 150 GW across the GCC
- **Phase I**: Connects Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, Qatar (400 kV HVDC)
- **Phase II**: Connects UAE and Oman (220 kV)

### Strategic Importance

- GCC states are among the world's highest per-capita electricity consumers (air conditioning)
- The interconnection prevents blackouts during peak demand
- Future plans include connecting the GCC grid to Iraq and potentially to Egypt/Jordan
