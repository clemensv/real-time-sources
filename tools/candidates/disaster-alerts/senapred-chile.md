## What It Provides

SENAPRED (formerly ONEMI until 2023) is Chile's national disaster management agency. Chile faces extreme multi-hazard exposure:

- **Earthquakes**: World's most seismically active country (see chile-csn.md)
- **Tsunamis**: Generated by Pacific subduction events; Pacific-wide propagation
- **Volcanic eruptions**: 90 active volcanoes (see sernageomin-chile.md)
- **Flooding**: Andean snowmelt + coastal storms
- **Landslides**: Steep Andean terrain + glacial lake outburst floods (GLOFs)
- **Wildfires**: Central Chile (Mediterranean climate) — devastating 2023 fires killed 137 people
- **Drought**: Central and northern Chile under multi-decade megadrought

SENAPRED operates the SAE (Sistema de Alerta de Emergencia) — Chile's cell broadcast emergency alert system, similar to the US Wireless Emergency Alert (WEA).

### Alert Levels

- **Alerta Temprana Preventiva**: Early warning / awareness
- **Alerta Amarilla**: Yellow alert — increased readiness
- **Alerta Roja**: Red alert — emergency in progress
- **Evacuación**: Evacuation order

## Integration Notes

- Chile's SAE cell broadcast system is one of the most tested in the world (regular drills due to earthquake/tsunami risk)
- A CAP alert feed from SENAPRED would be extremely valuable
- Chile's 2010 M8.8 earthquake and tsunami response failures led to the creation of modern alert systems
- The 2015 Calbuco eruption demonstrated real-time volcanic alerting
- WMO Alert Hub may include Chilean CAP alerts
