## Context

Cambodia and Laos are the two lower Mekong countries most affected by upstream dam construction and most dependent on the river's natural flood pulse for food security and livelihoods.

### Cambodia — Tonle Sap

Cambodia's **Tonle Sap** lake is one of the world's most productive freshwater ecosystems. Its unique reversal system — the Tonle Sap River reverses flow during monsoon, flooding the lake to 6x its dry-season size — is threatened by upstream Chinese and Laotian dams. Real-time monitoring of the Tonle Sap water level and flow reversal timing is ecologically critical.

Data sources:
- **MRC monitoring stations**: Covered by MRC portal (see existing candidate)
- **Cambodia's Ministry of Water Resources**: No API found
- **DHRW (Department of Hydrology and River Works)**: Limited digital presence
- **SERVIR-Mekong (NASA/USAID)**: Satellite-based flood monitoring — may have APIs

### Laos — Hydropower Dams

Laos is building itself as the "Battery of Southeast Asia" with 60+ planned/operating dams on Mekong tributaries. The 2018 Xe-Pian Xe-Namnoy dam collapse killed 49+ people and displaced thousands. Real-time dam monitoring is both an engineering necessity and a human safety issue.

Data sources:
- **Electricité du Laos (EdL)**: No public API
- **Nam Theun 2 (NTPC)**: Largest operating dam; environmental monitoring data exists but not public
- **MRC Flash Flood Guidance**: May cover Laos tributaries

## Verdict

Critical ecological/safety data, zero accessibility. The Tonle Sap reversal and Laotian dam monitoring are among the most important real-time data needs in Southeast Asia. The MRC portal provides partial coverage, but country-level data from Cambodia and Laos remains inaccessible. SERVIR-Mekong (NASA/USAID) may be a satellite-based alternative worth investigating.
