## What It Would Provide

The Toktogul Dam on the Naryn River (Syr Darya basin) in Kyrgyzstan is the linchpin of Central Asian water politics. This single reservoir controls water flow for:
- **Kyrgyzstan**: Hydropower generation (~90% of Kyrgyz electricity)
- **Uzbekistan**: Irrigation water for cotton agriculture (world's 6th largest producer)
- **Kazakhstan**: Water for southern agricultural regions
- **Tajikistan/Turkmenistan**: Indirectly affected through Syr Darya system

### The Central Asian Water-Energy Nexus

The fundamental tension: Kyrgyzstan wants to release water in winter (for hydropower when demand peaks) while Uzbekistan/Kazakhstan need water in summer (for irrigation). This conflict has nearly triggered military confrontations.

Key infrastructure:
- **Toktogul Dam**: 19.5 km³ reservoir; 1,200 MW hydroelectric capacity
- **Nurek Dam** (Tajikistan): 10.5 km³ on Vakhsh River (Amu Darya); 3,000 MW
- **Rogun Dam** (Tajikistan): Under construction; will be world's tallest dam (335m)
- **Kambarata-1** (Kyrgyzstan): Planned upstream of Toktogul

### Data Availability

Real-time reservoir levels and discharge data for Toktogul and other Central Asian dams appears to be:
- Shared bilaterally between Central Asian states through ICWC (Interstate Commission for Water Coordination)
- Occasionally published in news reports and government bulletins
- Not available through any public API
