## What It Provides

Myanmar faces extreme weather hazards — the 2008 Cyclone Nargis killed ~140,000 people, making it one of the deadliest natural disasters in Asian history. DMH monitors:

- **Cyclones**: Bay of Bengal cyclones approaching Myanmar's 2,000 km coastline
- **Monsoon rainfall**: Southwest monsoon brings extreme rainfall (June-October)
- **Flooding**: Irrawaddy/Ayeyarwady River flooding affects millions annually
- **Earthquakes**: Myanmar sits on the Sagaing Fault (highly active)
- **Marine weather**: Bay of Bengal and Andaman Sea conditions
- **Drought**: Central dry zone (Mandalay region)

### Probe Results

Not directly probed. Myanmar's web infrastructure may be affected by political instability since the 2021 coup and international sanctions. DMH is expected to be operational but with limited digital capabilities.

### Regional Significance

- Myanmar is the most cyclone-vulnerable country in the Bay of Bengal after Bangladesh
- The Irrawaddy Delta (Myanmar's rice bowl) floods annually
- Jade mining regions in Kachin State face devastating landslides
- Myanmar's seismic risk (Sagaing Fault) is undermonitored relative to its danger

## Verdict

Critical humanitarian data, severe political barriers. Myanmar's disaster vulnerability (Cyclone Nargis precedent) makes weather monitoring life-or-death, but political instability limits data accessibility. RIMES and Indian IMD provide partial cyclone coverage for the Bay of Bengal. Documented as an important gap with unique challenges.
